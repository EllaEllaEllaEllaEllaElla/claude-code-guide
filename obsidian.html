Obsidian

A really cool app — but its main job is simple.

When Claude creates a .md file, it's just text — your computer stores it as raw characters with symbols like # and -. Obsidian acts as a translator: it reads those symbols and turns them into something that actually looks nice — headings, bullets, bold text. You can also write notes in Obsidian and it saves them as .md files, which means Claude can read them right back. It's a two-way street between you and your files.
1

Download Obsidian

  1. Go to obsidian.md and click Download for Mac
  2. Open the downloaded file and drag Obsidian into your Applications folder
  3. Open it (right-click → Open the first time)
Free. No account needed.
2

Open your projects folder as a vault

  1. When Obsidian opens, click Open folder as vault
  2. Navigate to your Desktop and select your projects folder
  3. Click Open
A "vault" is just Obsidian's word for a folder it's reading. By pointing it at projects, every .md file Claude creates in any of your projects will automatically show up here — beautifully formatted, no extra steps.
3

Try it: create a file with Claude, edit in Obsidian

  1. In VS Code, create a new folder inside projects called test and open it
  2. Click ⚡ to open Claude and type:
create a file called notes.md with a short list of healthy meal ideas
  1. Switch to Obsidian — you'll see test/notes.md appear in the sidebar. Click it to open it.
  2. It looks nice, right? Now add a note to any item by typing something in brackets, like: [make this vegetarian] or [add a recipe for this one]
  3. Go back to VS Code, open Claude, and type:
look at notes.md and fix anything I've written in brackets
  1. Watch Obsidian update in real time as Claude edits the file
